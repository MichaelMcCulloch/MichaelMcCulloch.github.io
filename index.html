Autonomous Assembly of Neural Structure (AANS)
Dissipative, continual learning on silicon
Active Project

Summary

AANS is a constructive proof of the free energy principle implemented as a dissipative neural system. Instead of relying on backpropagation and replay buffers, the network continuously updates its structure and weights through interaction with its environment.

  • Online, lifelong, experiential learning with no catastrophic forgetting.
  • Sub‑50 ms latency at full GPU saturation, no settling period.
  • Structural credit assignment with >97% Pearson correlation vs eligibility propagation.
  • Lunar Lander in < 30 episodes on average, no replay buffers or rollouts.

Why it matters

If AGI is not a chatbot, then its substrate needs to learn continuously, under constraint, from experience. AANS is a step toward that: one model instance, one lifetime, no retraining from scratch.

Focus Areas

Self‑Organizing Neural Architectures

Architectures that build and revise themselves through interaction, borrowing from watershed formation, cellular metabolism, and atmospheric dynamics.

Silicon‑Native Intelligence

Treating CPUs/GPUs not as proxies for brains, but as a different substrate with different affordances: high precision, high power, global clocks, and vast memory. The goal is not a better brain, but a different class of cognitive system that fully exploits silicon.

Abstraction, Compilers, and LLMs

Understanding large language models as the next major compiler layer: tools that subsume rote skill while amplifying the remaining scarce resource—discernment. LLMs are not the end of programming; they are the latest abstraction over “how” so humans can spend more effort on “what” and “why.”

Thermodynamics of Learning

Viewing biological and artificial intelligence as dissipative structures: entities that persist by consuming low entropy and exporting high entropy. From this lens, learning becomes an energy‑driven process embedded in physical law, not just an optimization problem.

Selected Ideas

LLMs as the Next Compiler

A historical and conceptual walk from punch cards → assemblers → compilers → virtual machines → LLMs as the latest abstraction over “how”, freeing humans to focus on “what” and “why.” Assemblers didn’t kill programming. Compilers didn’t kill programming. LLMs won’t either—they just change which skills are scarce.

Silicon vs. Biology

The brain is an energy‑efficient accident under constraint; silicon is a high‑energy, high‑precision substrate that doesn’t share those constraints. The future of AI is not “a better brain” but an alien class of cognition exploiting speed, precision, and scale in ways biology could never afford.

Internet v1 → v3

A lament for the early, human‑scale internet of personal sites, Newgrounds, Neopets, and weird forums— and an observation that today’s web is closer to a chain of ghost towns where LLMs inhabit the empty storefronts left behind by people.

Risk, AGI, and “If Anyone Builds It…”

Reflections on AI risk framed as game theory plus human hubris, not science fiction. From Stockfish vs. 8 billion humans to the atom, flight, and space travel, the pattern is the same: “impossible” quietly becomes “inevitable” once the enabling technology appears.

Quantum Measurement vs. Quantum Woo

Clarifying that no physicist believes consciousness collapses the wavefunction; the measurement problem is about formalizing what “measurement” even means in a theory, not about human intent altering reality through vibes.

First Principles

First Principles over Rote Memorization

If I can’t derive a result from scratch, I don’t claim to understand it. This applies equally to calculus, CUDA kernels, and organizational dynamics.

Depth × Breadth = Volume

The most useful work comes from combining local depth with global context. I try to maximize the volume under that curve rather than specializing narrowly or generalizing shallowly.

Tools as Abstractions, Not Identity

I’m not a “Java person” or a “Python person.” Tools are ephemeral; taste and discernment are the actual skills. LLMs are simply the latest abstraction that frees humans from rote work while making good judgment more valuable.

Entropy as a Design Constraint

Whether building cloud ML infrastructure or neuromorphic‑style systems, I aim for minimal surface area exposed to entropy—a kind of no‑hair theorem for software.

Thermodynamic View of Systems

From economies to intelligence, I tend to see systems as flows of energy and information under constraint: billionaires as singularities, institutions as accretion disks, AI clusters as new kinds of stars.
