Theme

I study how systems like hurricanes, cells, brains, and silicon networks maintain structure by exporting entropy — and how to turn that into a practical recipe for online learning.

Prigogine gives us dissipative systems. Friston gives us the Free Energy Principle. My work is in the gap between them: going from “what” to “how” — exact credit assignment, in real time, with only local information.

Autonomous Assembly of Neural Structure (AANS)

AANS is a dissipative implementation of the Free Energy Principle: a neural architecture that grows and prunes itself while interacting with an environment, performing structural, spatial, and temporal credit assignment online.

  • Sub‑30 episode Lunar Lander, no replay buffers or rollouts.
  • >97% Pearson correlation with eligibility propagation for structural credit assignment.
  • 0% catastrophic forgetting in tested regimes.
  • Sub‑50 ms latency at full GPU saturation, no settling period.

It behaves less like a trained model and more like a physical process embedded in silicon.

LLMs as the Next Compiler

“Is the LLM Really the Next Compiler?”

Punch cards → assemblers → compilers → virtual machines → large language models. Each step automated “how” and elevated “what.” LLMs don’t end programming; they subsume rote skill. Taste — which tool, for which problem — becomes the scarce resource.

Silicon vs. Biology

Brains evolved under hard energy constraints. They are masterpieces of efficiency and adequate computation. Silicon is a different territory entirely: tensor cores, CUDA, fibre, and grid power measured in megawatts, not millivolts.

The future of AI is not a better brain. It’s a different kind of mind: high‑energy, high‑precision, statistically immense, running nonstop in distributed clusters.

The Ghost Internet

Internet v1 was personal sites and weird corners. Internet v2 consolidated into platforms. Internet v3 is ghost towns and bots — LLMs talking to LLMs over abandoned storefronts where people used to be.

Risk & Inevitability

If all 8 billion humans played Stockfish, they’d all lose. The same story played out for Go, Stratego, Dota. “AI will never X” keeps aging badly. The control problem is math plus incentives, not vibes. We struggled with climate change; AGI won’t be easier.

First Principles

First Principles Over Rote

If I can’t derive it from scratch, I don’t claim to know it. That applies equally to calculus, CUDA kernels, and cloud architectures.

Depth × Breadth = Volume

Real leverage comes from holding both the micro and macro in mind. I aim for volume: enough depth to build, enough breadth to see the system around the thing I’m building.

Tools Are Not Identity

I am not a Java program. Languages, frameworks, and models are tools. The job is choosing the right abstraction under the right constraints. LLMs widen the tool surface; they don’t remove the need for discernment.

Entropy as a Design Partner

Whether I’m building infra or neuromorphic‑style systems, I treat entropy as a design parameter. Fewer moving parts, fewer failure modes. Minimal hair.
